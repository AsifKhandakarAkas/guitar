Basic Guitar chords – Beginner level

You may find lots of chord for playing guitar. But may be few of them really needs regularly and repeatedly and of course some of them are quite simple. In this post I’m going to list those guitar chords exclusively for beginner.

Basic chords for beginner

Why I select them as beginner level guitar chord?

These guitar chords are not only easy to learn but also these are the basic chords every guitar player should learn. These guitar chords are simultaneously using in most of the songs. If you are a beginner then don’t miss this discussion because I tried to make the list as a sequence of relatively easy to hard. I don’t want to puzzle you and I wish it could be easy and understandable for you. Okay let’s jump on it.

E minor

This is the simplest guitar chord I think. And it’s very much suitable for you to learn even if you are an absolute beginner. All you have to do just place two finger on fret board and these are one finger on 2nd fret 4th string and another finger on 5th string and same fret. Isn’t it super easy!!!

E major

Let’s introduce another easy chord to you. Once you are comfortable with E minor chord then it will be easy to hold this E major chord. You just have to add one extra finger with previous E minor chord. So final position is that one finger is on 3rd string 1st fret, one on 4th string 2nd fret and last finger on 5th string 3rd fret. Hope this chord seems easy to you. If not then don’t be panic after practicing couple of time it should be easier to you.

E7

You know how to hold E major chord!!! Then just put off your finger from 4th string and that it. So finger position is one finger on 3rd string 1st fret and 5th string 2nd fret.

A major

A major is one of the basic and most used chords while playing song. But fortunately it’s not that much of hard in fact it’s pretty easier to hold. What you have to do is just put your three fingers on 2nd, 3rd and 4th string and on same 2nd fret. You may find little bit difficulty to use your all three finger without buzzing but don’t worry you will do it over the time.

A minor

This A minor chord is similar with the chord E major in shape. Just step down one string from your holding of E major chord. So your finger position of A minor is one finger on 2nd string 1st fret and other finger is on 2nd fret of 3rd & 4th string.

A7

It’s pretty simple to hold A7 guitar chord. You just need two finger one finger on 2nd and 4th string of 3nd fret. Is it similar to A major chord!!!! One different is that middle finger is missing.

D major

D major is easy to hold but it’s quite tricky to hold and play. Great thing is that it sounds sweet. You have to put one finger on 3rd string 2nd fret, one finger on 1st string 2nd fret and last finger on 2nd string 3rd fret. Seems tricky right!!! Another tricky thing is that you shouldn’t strum upper two strings especially the upper one. Hope you will do it in couple of day.

G major

G major is one of the basic chords every guitar player may know. You need 3-4 fingers to play G major chord. I said 3 or 4 because you can skip holding 2nd string. Before playing G major chord on guitar for the first time, you need to do some finger stretching exercises even this is not going to be that much of hard. Your finger position is index finger on 3rd fret 6th string, middle finger on 2nd fret 5th string and lastly pinky finger on 3rd fret 1st string. Oh you can add another finger on 3rd fret 2nd string also.

D minor

D minor is also tricky as D major to play because when strumming this chord again you have to skip upper two strings. Finger position is one finger on 1st fret 1st string, one finger on 2nd fret 3rd string and last finger on 3rd fret 2nd string. You have to stretch your finger little bit.

C major

Hey it’s little bit harder to stretch your finger as a beginner. And it is important for you to have flexible finger for holding C major chord on guitar fret. So you have to do some exercise to use to with finger stretching for holding C major guitar chord. Finger position is one finger on 2nd string 1st fret, one finger on 4th string 2nd fret and last finger on 5th string 2nd fret as well.

D7

This is another easy chord. Put your finger on 2nd fret 1st string, one finger on 1st fret 2nd string and last finger on 2nd fret 3rd string. Now strum your guitar without touching upper two strings. That’s it.

C7

This is the toughest chord to play in this list. So you have to spend little bit more time with this chord. Don’t worry I’m here to explain you to make this easier to you. Place your finger in C major position. Now press 3rd string on 3rd fret with your pinky finger. That’s it!!! Follow the above picture to hold perfectly.

Okay these are some guitar chord you can learn as a beginner. There are so many songs you can find to play with these simple guitar chords. Just practice with patience and play some beautiful songs. Wish you Best of luck for your guitar journey.
